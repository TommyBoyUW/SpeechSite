Speech-Language Pathologists (SLPs) work to prevent, evaluate, diagnose, and treat speech, language, social communication, cognitive-communication, and swallowing disorders in both children and adults. In addition, SLPs provide training and education to families, caregivers, and other professionals. SLPs work collaboratively with professionals from many other disciplines including Occupational Therapy and Physical Therapy.
